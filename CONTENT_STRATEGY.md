# Content Strategy

> Lightweight strategy anchor for content prioritization and routing decisions.
> Referenced by `/write` (Phase 1), `/content` command, and daily review content triage.

---

## Topic pillars

Three areas where Tim has unique, practical insights. All content should map to one of these.

### 1. AI + content operations
How we actually use AI at Animalz — process, tooling, results, failures. Real production examples, not theory.

*Examples: how we built the AI content engine, Claude Code for content marketers, AEO/SEO with AI*

### 2. Building with AI
What I'm building with Claude Code and why — personal OS, writing workflows, agent systems, developer tools. Building in public with real learnings.

*Examples: context engineering, CLI for vibe coders, content system design*

### 3. Working differently with AI
How AI is changing knowledge work, practically — real patterns, real tradeoffs, real productivity insights. Grounded in experience, not speculation.

*Examples: the multitasking trap with AI, focused work in 2026, agent-native workflows*

## Publication routing

### Decision tree

| Question | Channel |
|---|---|
| For Animalz's audience, in company voice? | **Animalz Blog** (SEO/editorial) or **Animalz KB** (playbook/docs/research) |
| Testing an idea for a larger piece? | **LinkedIn** (strategic, tracked to parent idea) |
| Deep dive, essay, or newsletter-length? | **We Eat Robots** (Substack) |
| Evergreen, portfolio-worthy, or workflow writeup? | **timmetz.nl** |
| Grey zone (personal take on Animalz-relevant topic)? | Personal channel first, adapt for Animalz later if it has legs |

### LinkedIn principle

LinkedIn posts should primarily serve as testing grounds for larger pieces. A post is worth writing when it:
- Tests an idea with a clear path to something more lasting, OR
- Captures a practical insight worth sharing that connects to a pillar

Avoid frivolous posts with no strategic purpose. When a post performs well, flag it for expansion.

**Exception:** A quick reaction post (< 5 minutes, sharing a quote or link with brief commentary) is fine occasionally — but even these should ideally connect to a pillar.

## Quality bar

| Channel | Minimum bar |
|---|---|
| **LinkedIn** | Passes voice check, has one clear insight, connects to a larger idea |
| **We Eat Robots** | Has original thinking, not just commentary on others' work |
| **Animalz Blog** | Serves an SEO goal or establishes a thought leadership position |
| **Animalz KB** | Actionable and reference-worthy — someone can use this to do something |
| **timmetz.nl** | Portfolio-quality, shows craft and depth |

---

## What to say no to

- **Philosophical/visionary AI takes** — "What AI means for society," AI bubble commentary, grand predictions. Park these for later when audience is larger.
- **General content marketing craft** — Not Tim's unique value. Others cover this better.
- **Standalone LinkedIn posts with no path forward** — If there's no connection to a pillar or a larger piece, it's not worth the time.
- **Reactive hot takes** — Unless it genuinely adds a unique practical perspective, don't chase the news cycle.

---

## Guiding principle

**Keep it practical.** People are waiting for unique, real-world insights on AI + content, not vision pieces. The biggest value I can provide right now is everything I'm finding out about how to use AI with content, how to be productive with AI, how to use Claude Code. That's where I can deliver unique and practical insights that people appreciate.
