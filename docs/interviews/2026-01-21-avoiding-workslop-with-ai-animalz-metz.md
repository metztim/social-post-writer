# The Autonomous Marketer: How Tim Metz Creates High-Quality Internal Docs With the Help of His “Unslop” Command

## In case you missed it

Merriam-Webster’s word of the year for 2025 was **“slop”**: “digital content of low quality that is produced usually in quantity by means of artificial intelligence.”

One increasingly common subtype is **workslop**—internal work documents that seem valuable at first glance but collapse under closer inspection.

> “It seems valuable when you first read it, but then if you really try to understand what it says, you realize it has no value or just doesn’t make any sense.” — Tim Metz

## How Animalz uses AI deliberately

Animalz embraces AI across both client-facing and internal workflows—but deliberately.

> “You really have to build almost a different infrastructure, giving the AI models access to different kinds of data…” — Tim Metz

**How AI is used at Animalz:**
- **High-end thought leadership & research:** Minimal AI
- **LinkedIn content programs:** Heavy AI infrastructure with human oversight
- **AEO audits:** Significant AI assistance

> “I don't think all work can ever be done by AI… but more and more of it can be done by AI than ever before.”

## How to build an “unslop” command

Even well-designed AI systems produce slop—sometimes inside the system itself.

> “Even if you use AI behind the scenes… it tends to produce workslop.”

To combat this, Metz developed a custom Claude prompt called the **“Unslop” command**, based on three principles.

### Principle 1: MECE (Mutually Exclusive, Collectively Exhaustive)

Ensure sections cover everything without overlap.

> “You haven't left anything out, but you also haven't made different sections overlap each other.”

### Principle 2: DRY (Don’t Repeat Yourself)

If something has been said once, it shouldn’t be repeated.

### Principle 3: Make things as simple as possible—but not simpler

Avoid cutting essential examples or context.

> “If it starts to read like shorthand, you’ve gone too far.”

Every change is tested against standardized workflows to ensure quality hasn’t regressed.

## The “Unslop” command in action

The command:
1. Identifies sections to cut (with justification)
2. Recommends sections to relocate
3. Rewrites the document without slop
4. Analyzes reduction and applied principles

The result: documents that surface decisions clearly and quickly.

## Key takeaways

- Build AI infrastructure before scaling
- Create feedback loops from outputs
- Keep humans in the loop
- Apply clear editing principles (MECE, DRY)
- Watch for slop everywhere—especially internal docs
- Match AI usage to content type

> “Most people are not really trying to cheat with AI; they just need a shortcut.”
