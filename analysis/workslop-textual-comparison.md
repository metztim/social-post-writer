## Key Insights About User's Voice

### 1. **Show, Don't Preach**
The deletion of "AI can be powerful for collaboration. But this isn't collaboration—it's cognitive burden-shifting" is telling. The user trusts the audience to understand the concept without editorial commentary. They present the problem and let readers draw their own conclusions.

### 2. **Avoid Direct Accusations**
Deleting "You used AI to save yourself time by not thinking. I lose time figuring out what you meant." shows the user avoids alienating readers. Instead of pointing fingers, they create a shared experience ("you've been workslopped").

### 3. **Conversational, Not Casual**
The user is conversational ("That's not all though," "congrats") but not sloppy. They maintain professional credibility with specific sources while using playful irony to engage.

### 4. **White Space = Emotional Rhythm**
Line breaks aren't just formatting - they create emotional buildup:
```
The content is vague.
Key details are missing.
You're not sure what you're supposed to do with this.
```
This staccato rhythm builds frustration more effectively than a single paragraph.

### 5. **Simple, Powerful CTAs**
"Have you been workslopped recently?" is far more powerful than "My question for today: have you encountered workslop at work? How did you handle it?" One question. Direct. Memorable.

### 6. **Source Credibility Matters**
User adds "BetterUp Labs and Stanford" and "(Full HBR article in the comments.)" - they want readers to know this is backed by research, not just opinion.

## Conclusion

The user's editorial style is characterized by **strategic deletion** rather than addition. They cut anything that:
1. Tells the reader how to feel (editorializing)
2. Accuses the reader (alienating)
3. Over-explains (condescending)
4. Adds meta-commentary (distracting)

What remains is **conversational, credible, and emotionally resonant**. The user trusts the concept to land without heavy-handed delivery. They use white space and rhythm to control emotion, playful irony to engage, and specific sources to establish authority.

**Key takeaway:** When drafting for this user, err on the side of showing rather than telling, and trust them to cut what doesn't serve the core message. They have a sharp editorial eye for what's essential.
