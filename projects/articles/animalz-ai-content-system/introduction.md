# Introduction plan

## Hook
- Reader has moved past ChatGPT subscriptions and shared prompt templates — they know that's not serious
- They're looking at workflow tools (AirOps, similar). That feels like the answer.
- Pattern interrupt: it's not.

## Sinker
- What you actually need is a system. Here's what that looked like when we built ours.
- Reference the AI onion lightly — we'd already named the phenomenon. This article is what it looks like in practice.

## Line
- Journey from "wishful workflow thinking" to "systems thinking"
- Each section reveals another way reality diverges from the wishful version
- By the end, the reader has shifted mindset: the last problem (keeping people sharp) only exists because the system works — proof they've arrived at systems thinking
- Not a metaphor to carry; a perspective shift that builds through the structure

## Callback points
1. Each H2 implicitly answers "why wishful workflow thinking fails" from a different angle
2. Conclusion lands the full shift: you're now thinking in systems, and systems need sharp humans
