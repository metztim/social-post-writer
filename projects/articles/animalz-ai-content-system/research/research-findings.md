## Parking lot: material to draw from during drafting

### Trust-flip / automation complacency (conclusion)

**The Glass Cage — Nicholas Carr:**
- "Both complacency and bias tend to become more severe as the quality and reliability of an automated system improve. Experiments show that when a system produces errors fairly frequently, we stay on high alert... But when a system is more reliable, breaking down or making mistakes only occasionally, we get lazy."
- "Automation tends to turn us from actors into observers."
- "What if the cost of machines that think is people who don't?" — George Dyson, 2008
- "Sometimes I wonder if we made an airplane that is too easy to fly... because in a difficult airplane the crews may stay more alert." — Airbus executive to William Langewiesche
- "Sharp tools, dull minds." — Vivek Haldar (Google engineer)
- "The more accurate the machine gets, the lazier the questions become." — Amit Singhal (Google)

**Ezra Klein on Lex Fridman (#462):**
- "My biggest concern about AI is we're going to make ourselves much stupider without realizing it. Because the things that are easy to automate in AI... that's where all the intelligence happens, in my view."
- Lex: "I'm definitely becoming a worse programmer by using AI, offloading, because it actually works really well there."

**Deskilling (Two Worlds page):**
- "Many mid-level jobs will be turned into low-skilled jobs because of automation (so-called deskilling; think of a London cab driver who had to know the entire map of London by head. Now a smartphone will suffice)."

### Customer experience

**Who Is Michael Ovitz? — CAA white-glove model:**
- When you drove in, "one of our parking concierges (who'd been given a photo of you) would open your car door, greet you with a big smile... An assistant would escort you to the conference room, where five to ten poised, well-dressed agents would be waiting. They'd already have had a premeeting to script the ideas we'd be presenting."
- Parallel: intake form pre-fill shows the customer you've done your homework before they arrive.

**Build — Tony Fadell:**
- "The actual thing you're building is only one tiny part of a vast, intangible, overlooked user journey that starts long before a customer ever gets their hands on your product and ends long after."

**Dynamic Context Problem — Carta/First Round:**
- "We focused on workflows where human judgement was essential but inefficient, situations where delays or inconsistencies in judgement degraded client experience."

### Systems vs. tools / workflows

**Artifact thinking (Every — "Creative Work Is About to Look a Lot More Like Programming"):**
- "While prompts disappear the moment you close the tab, workflows become assets."
- "Artifact thinking: creative work that produces discrete outputs, one at a time, each beginning from scratch." — Useful contrast: workflow = artifact thinking. System = something more.

**Leverage Points — Donella Meadows:**
- "Physical structure is crucial in a system, but rarely a leverage point, because changing it is rarely quick or simple. The leverage point is in proper design in the first place."
- "Delays in feedback loops are critical determinants of system behavior."

**Clear Thinking — Shane Parrish:**
- "It's about designing systems when you're at your best that work for you when you're at your worst."

### Compounding / feedback loops

**Teaching Smart People How to Learn — HBR:**
- Single-loop vs. double-loop learning: "a thermostat that automatically turns on the heat whenever the temperature drops... A double-loop thermostat would be one that could ask, 'Why am I set at 68 degrees?'" — System with Ordinal data is double-loop.

**The Workflows Turning One Engineer Into Ten — Every:**
- "When you're following the principles of compounding engineering — building development systems that learn from your feedback — every workflow improvement makes the next one easier."

**Way of the Champion — Belichick/Rabil:**
- "The number-one rule of compounding, they say, is to not interrupt."

### Intro / framing

**VOC from Ty's sales calls:**
- "AirOps is like giving a Formula 1 car to someone who just got their driver's license."
- "Oh, it's really slow" (ClickUp customer, months in, still struggling)
- "AirOps says they will help us set it up well... but is that really going to happen?"
- "Each article required 2-3 hours of heavy editing to sound human."

**Two failure modes (positioning doc):**
- Over-AI it: slop at scale. Google March 2024 deindexed 800+ sites.
- Under-build it: tool gathering dust. MIT 2025: 95% of AI pilots fail.

**Stats:**
- AI-generated posts get 45% less engagement (Originality.AI)
- Consumer enthusiasm for AI content dropped 60% → 26%, 2023-2025 (eMarketer)
- 95% of AI pilots fail to reach production scale (MIT)
- 64% use AI, only 18% see competitive advantage (HubSpot)
- Vendor partnerships succeed 67% vs. 33% internal builds (MIT)

### Other potentially useful

**Animalz internal ("Animalz Approach to AI"):**
