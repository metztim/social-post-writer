# Image Plan — Your Content Deserves More Than Wishful Workflow Thinking

## Style direction

**Middle ground:** More narrative/figurative than the context engineering article's abstract-tech illustrations, but grounded in Animalz brand colors and feel. Think: illustrated, slightly whimsical, but professional. Not a literal children's fairy tale — more like editorial illustration with warmth and personality.

**Palette (from brand guidelines):**
- Primary: Purple-500 (#9463EE), Purple-700 (#6933CC)
- Secondary: Blue-500 (#0CAFDF), Teal-500 (#32C8BE)
- Accents: Pink-500 (#EF6C9C), Peach-500 (#FE844E), Yellow-500 (#FFD400)
- Background: Light/white or Purple-50 (#F5F0FF) / Blue-50 (#E6F8FE)

**Reference touchpoints:**
- Context engineering article hero: The primary style reference. Rich digital illustration with depth, dimension, lighting, and detail — not flat or minimal. Uses the brand purple/blue palette but as a full illustration, not decorative graphics.
- Animalz critters: Decorative brand elements only (simple line drawings). Do NOT base illustration style on these — they're too minimal for editorial images. Could appear as subtle Easter eggs within an illustration, but they don't define the rendering approach.
- Publish button campaign image: Physical, tactile, weighty (concept reference, not style reference)

**Aspect ratios:**
- Featured: 16:9 (1200x675 or similar for blog hero)
- Inline: 3:2 or 16:9 (consistency with featured)

## Image 1: Featured — River into machine

**Concept:** A gentle, organic river of colorful content (floating documents, text snippets, ideas represented as glowing shapes) flows from the left side into an increasingly complex but beautiful machine on the right. The left side is natural and organic — soft gradients, flowing water. As the river enters the machine, it transforms: pipes, gears, connected databases, routers appear, all rendered in the Animalz purple/blue/teal palette. The machine isn't cold or industrial — it's elegant, almost like a Rube Goldberg device with purpose. Warm lighting. The overall feeling: what starts as a simple flow becomes something unexpectedly complex and beautiful.

**Prompt for Gemini (Round 1):**

> Image 1 is the color palette reference. Image 2 is the style reference — match the richness, depth, dimension, and lighting quality of this illustration. This is a full editorial illustration, not a flat or minimal graphic.
>
> Create a wide landscape illustration (16:9 aspect ratio) showing a gentle river of content flowing from left to right into an elegant, layered machine. On the left side, the river is organic and natural — soft flowing water in light blue (#0CAFDF) and teal (#32C8BE) tones, carrying floating abstract shapes that suggest documents, text, and ideas. As the river flows right, it enters a sophisticated but warm mechanical system: interconnected pipes, small gear mechanisms, database containers, and routing pathways, all rendered in purple (#9463EE and #6933CC) with blue and teal accents. The machine is not cold or industrial — it has an almost whimsical quality, like a beautifully designed system where every piece connects to the next. The background is very light, almost white with subtle purple (#F5F0FF) warmth. Soft, warm lighting from the upper left. The style is rich editorial illustration with depth and dimension — detailed rendering, soft gradients, volumetric lighting, modern and professional but with personality and warmth. Not flat or minimal. No text in the image.

## Image 2: Publish button

**Concept:** An illustrated version of the campaign's "PUBLISH" button. A large, satisfying, well-worn mechanical button with "PUBLISH" stamped on it. Rendered in the article's illustrated style rather than photorealistic. The button should feel physical and weighty — something you'd want to press — but drawn, not photographed. Slight wear marks suggest it's been pressed many times. This is the goal: a publish button that feels earned.

**Prompt for Gemini (Round 1):**

> Image 1 is the color palette reference. Image 2 is a concept reference showing the physical button idea — I want a similar concept but in an illustrated style, not photorealistic.
>
> Create an illustration of a large, satisfying mechanical "PUBLISH" button, viewed from a slight angle above. The button is chunky and physical — a raised rectangular key or industrial push-button with the word "PUBLISH" stamped or embossed on its surface. The button is rendered in warm cream and light gray tones with subtle purple (#9463EE) glow or accent lighting around its edges, suggesting it's powered up and ready to press. Slight wear marks and patina on the button surface show it has been pressed many times before. The background is clean and minimal — very light with soft shadows beneath the button. The style is editorial illustration: clean lines, gentle shading, modern but warm. Not photorealistic — clearly illustrated but with satisfying physical dimension. The word "PUBLISH" should be clearly readable on the button face in a clean, slightly worn typeface.

## Image 3: Nathan the dragon slayer

**Concept:** A cartoonized version of Nathan — recognizable if you know him (light brown hair styled up, blue-green eyes, stubble, warm expression) but clearly an illustration, not a photo. He's wielding an oversized red editing pen like a sword, facing off against a "slop dragon" — a comical, not-scary creature made of generic AI-generated text, lorem ipsum, and corporate buzzwords. The dragon is dissolving/disintegrating where Nathan's pen strikes it. Playful, heroic, tongue-in-cheek.

**Prompt for Gemini (Round 1):**

> Image 1 is a photo of the person this character should be based on — create a cartoonized, illustrated version that captures his likeness (light brown hair styled upward, blue-green eyes, light stubble, warm confident smile) but is clearly a stylized illustration. Image 2 is the color palette reference.
>
> Create a playful editorial illustration of a cartoonized young man based on the reference photo, depicted as a heroic editor-warrior. He wears a casual outfit (denim or similar) and wields a giant red editing pen like a sword, held confidently in a dynamic but not aggressive pose. He faces a comical "slop dragon" — a bumbling, not-scary creature whose body appears to be made of swirling generic text, corporate buzzwords, and AI-generated filler content. Where the red pen touches the dragon, the text dissolves into clean, sparkling particles. The dragon looks more confused than menacing. The overall tone is tongue-in-cheek and heroic, like a playful book illustration. Color palette: the character and pen in warm tones, the dragon in muted grays and washed-out purples that contrast with the vibrant Animalz purple (#9463EE) and teal (#32C8BE) background accents. Light, clean background with subtle warmth. The style bridges editorial illustration and storybook art — professional but with personality and humor. Aspect ratio 3:2 landscape.

**Reference images to attach:**
1. `nathan.jpeg` (likeness reference)
2. `animalz-colors.png` (color palette)

**Notes:** Character likeness from photos is hit-or-miss with Nano Banana Pro. Plan for 2-3 rounds of iteration on face/hair details. If likeness doesn't work, can fall back to a more stylized/abstract figure that's "clearly an editor type" without needing to be Nathan specifically.
