## Featured Image — Concept A: "Building the right environment"

Aspect ratio: 16:9 (blog hero placement)

### Prompt

A wide-format illustrated scene in an isometric perspective on a warm cream background. The scene shows an intricate, layered information architecture: neatly organized shelves and cubes containing labeled elements like documents, charts, data flows, and reference materials, all connected by elegant thin lines suggesting orchestration and flow. Lush botanical elements — tropical leaves, vines, seed pods — grow organically through and around the geometric structures, suggesting something alive and thriving.

A small platypus sits perched on one of the upper shelves, observing the scene — a decorative accent, not the focal point.

The illustration style is hand-drawn line art with soft, muted fills. Use these specific colors from Image 1 (the color palette): purple (#9463EE and lighter #E9DFFB), teal (#32C8BE and lighter #D2F9F6), yellow (#FFD400 and lighter #FCF5CF), and peach (#FE844E) as accent. The line work should be in a dark purple (#6933CC). The overall mood is sophisticated, warm, and approachable — not corporate or sterile.

No text in the image. The composition should work well when cropped to the right half (since blog hero shows image on the right side of a dark card).

## Featured Image — Concept D: "Engineered context with flow"

Architecture-forward but with flowing energy. The middle ground between B's botanical excess and C's sterility.

Aspect ratio: 16:9 (blog hero placement)

Reference images to attach:
1. Brand guidelines color page (colors only)
2. Landing page hero illustration (mood/energy reference only — do NOT copy the botanical density or leaf shapes)

### Prompt

A wide-format illustrated scene on a warm cream (#F9F7F6) background, showing a visual transition from left to right. The style is hand-drawn line art with soft, muted color fills — the line work should feel loose and human, not technical or diagrammatic.

On the left quarter: a single clean prompt box drawn in light gray outline, with a few faint geometric shapes nearby — a small cube, a thin grid fragment, a couple of dotted lines drifting rightward. Sparse but composed.

Moving rightward: the architecture builds and comes alive. Isometric shelves, containers, and platforms appear, connected by flowing purple (#6933CC) lines. Documents, folder icons, charts, and data nodes fill the structures. But the connections between elements aren't just straight lines — they curve and flow with energy, like streams or ribbons of teal (#32C8BE) and purple (#9463EE) winding between the geometric forms. Some of these flowing lines have small organic details — a curl at the end, a gentle wave, a subtle flourish — giving the whole system a sense of movement and life.

On the right third: a dense, layered information architecture at full energy. Structures are stacked and interconnected, data flows visibly between them through those curving ribbon-like connections. Splashes of yellow (#FFD400) and peach (#FE844E) accent the documents and highlights. The flowing lines weave through and around the geometry, creating a sense of rhythm. A handful of small leaf or vine shapes (5-6 total) emerge naturally from the flowing lines — they feel like part of the movement, not decoration pasted on top.

The overall impression: someone built this system with care, and it's humming with activity. Engineered but alive. The transition from left to right is a gradient of density, color, and energy — no hard dividing line.

Line work in dark purple (#6933CC). Fills use lighter tints (#E9DFFB, #D2F9F6, #FCF5CF). The warm cream background should remain visible throughout — don't fill every gap.

The focal point of the composition is a large, prominent screen or document window — positioned at the center or slightly left of center, roughly 25-30% of the image height. This is the OUTPUT — the thing being produced. All the context architecture (shelves, documents, data nodes, flowing connections) surrounds it and flows INTO it from behind and around it, like a rich backstage feeding a clean stage. The screen itself is simple and mostly white/empty, contrasting with the dense, colorful context system behind it. This creates the visual story: all this engineered context produces one clean result.

No text in the image. No animals or characters. The composition should work well when cropped to the right half or right two-thirds (blog hero shows image on the right side of a dark card).

## In-Article — "Team Discipline: 4 Capabilities" (v2 magician's hat)

Aspect ratio: 4:3 (inline blog image)

Reference images to attach:
1. Featured image (style/mood reference — match the hand-drawn line art with flowing energy)
2. Animalz logo (for corner wordmark)
3. Brand guidelines critters page (for critter/animal illustration style)
4. Animalz brand fonts (for text rendering reference — may or may not help)

### Prompt

An illustrated diagram on a warm cream (#F9F7F6) background. The style matches Image 1 (the featured image): hand-drawn line art with soft muted color fills, loose line work in dark purple (#6933CC). Do NOT include any content from the reference images themselves — only use them for style.

The composition is vertical, centered, reading top to bottom:

**Top — the magic:** A classic magician's top hat sits at the top center of the image. A cute, friendly rabbit emerges from the hat, drawn in the same warm illustrated style as the critters in Image 3. The rabbit looks confident and polished — this is the clean, impressive output. The hat and rabbit together are labeled "Context Engineering" with a hand-drawn highlight marker style background in light purple (#E9DFFB) behind the text.

**Below the hat — the messy work:** Four colored strands or ribbons flow downward and outward from the base of the hat, spreading toward the bottom corners of the image. They're tangled, overlapping, weaving around each other — not neatly braided but organically intertwined, suggesting the complex behind-the-scenes work. Each strand is a distinct color and labeled:
- "Domain Expertise" — purple strand (#9463EE)
- "Editorial Judgment" — teal strand (#32C8BE)
- "Systems Thinking" — yellow strand (#FFD400)
- "AI Literacy" — peach strand (#FE844E)

The visual reads: from the audience's perspective, you see the rabbit (the magic, the clean result). But pull back the curtain and you see the four tangled capabilities that make it possible. The strands should feel energetic and alive — flowing with the same ribbon-like quality as the connections in the featured image, with curls, overlaps, and movement.

Font hierarchy: "Context Engineering" with its highlight is the most prominent label. The four capability labels are smaller, positioned along their respective strands. Animalz logo wordmark (matching Image 2) in the bottom right corner.
