# Brief: Animalz AI Services Landing Page

## Deliverable
Full landing page (hero + features + proof + CTA) for `animalz.co/ai-services`.

## Audience
Content or marketing leader (VP, Director, CMO) at a B2B SaaS company. Two sub-segments, both decision-makers with budget:

**Early evaluators:** Seriously considering AirOps or similar, sensing it's a bigger undertaking than just buying a tool. Looking for someone to help them get it right from the start. Worried about spending big on a magic bullet that isn't — if the tool investment doesn't deliver, their reputation takes the hit.

**Stuck owners:** Bought AirOps, can't get content worth publishing out of it. AirOps helps with onboarding but can't deeply customize or build the surrounding systems. Stuck with a product they've purchased and can't fully leverage.

**Shared context:** Pressure from above and across the org to make AI work for content. Others are convinced content can now be done by AI — AirOps billboards, hype cycle. If the leader delivers, they're a hero. If it fails, same people will blame them. Additional pressure to solve AEO (70%+ of Animalz inbound is now AEO requests).

## Arrival paths
- **SEO/AEO search** ("AirOps implementation partner," "AI content workflows agency"): Highest intent, actively searching for help.
- **LinkedIn post** by Animalz or team member: Curious, intrigued by something specific. Medium intent.
- **Blog/newsletter:** Already in Animalz orbit, some trust established. Exploring what's actually offered.

One page handles all three. Emotional state is similar across paths; difference is intent level, which the page structure naturally accommodates.

## Attitude shift
**Point A:** "We need to make AI work for content — there's pressure from above, pressure from AEO, maybe pressure to justify an AirOps investment. But if we spend big on a tool and it doesn't deliver, that's on me. My team isn't equipped to build this, and I'm not confident the tool alone will get us there."

**Point B:** "This is a systems-building problem, not a content problem — and Animalz has already solved it at production scale. We should talk to them."

## "This is an ad about..."
This is a landing page about Animalz's AI implementation services for content/marketing leaders at B2B SaaS companies who need to make AI content production work at scale. The goal of the page is to make them book a call.

## Constraints
- **Voice:** Animalz brand voice — confident, concrete, warm. Authority through specificity, approachability through storytelling and transparency. Room to sharpen.
- **Density:** High VPW (value per word). Dense and punchy. Every word earns its place.
- **CTA:** "Book a call" or similar conversational phrasing.
- **Pricing:** Include for now (Advise $25-50K, Build $75-250K, Manage $15-25K/mo). May simplify or remove later.
- **Client names:** Likely can't use. Lean on metrics and internal proof instead.
- **AirOps:** Prominent dedicated section per positioning strategy.
- **Team faces:** Consider featuring Tim Metz and Johnson Sun as the AI team leads. Risk: two people may seem small. Mitigate with "led by" framing and capacity proof via metrics.
- **No CMS/template constraints** — write the copy, figure out layout later.
