- "Oh, it's really slow" (ClickUp customer, started Oct/Nov, months later still struggling)

## AirOps implementation pain (web research)

### Steep learning curve
- "The first time I used it, I was overwhelmed. There's a lot happening on screen."
- "AirOps is like giving a Formula 1 car to someone who just got their driver's license."
- "User feedback on G2 consistently highlights a steep learning curve. While it's a no-code platform, building effective workflows requires a significant time investment."
- "2-4 weeks to reach basic proficiency, with true mastery taking 2-3 months."

### Implementation is a time sink
- "Creating a simple blog post workflow required connecting 8+ steps" and "took nearly four hours."
- "You spend hours learning AirOps' workflow builder, only to discover the final article needs 2-3 hours of heavy editing."
- "I burned through half of my 1,000 monthly tasks just figuring out how to build my first workflow properly."
- Users become "prompt engineers debugging why your third conditional step keeps breaking."

### Post-sale neglect
- "After signing the contract, there was zero onboarding or welcome communication."
- "Support acknowledged a problem, confirmed it needed escalation, and explicitly told us someone would follow up the same day, but that follow-up never happened — it's now been over two weeks."

### Pricing confusion
- "Task-based billing just makes it too complicated to predict your monthly bill."
- One 2,000-word article consumed "approximately 2,500-3,500 tasks" — making costs unpredictable.
- "'Contact sales' model makes it difficult for teams to budget and compare costs without engaging in a sales process."

### Output quality after all that effort
- "Sounds very AI-written with repetitive phrasing, generic transitions, shallow analysis, weak introductions."
- "Each article required 2-3 hours of heavy editing to sound human."
- Quality score averaged 3.1/5 across testing.
- Platform "takes brand guidelines to an extreme," removing necessary negative framing, "messes with the structure and formatting."

## Broader AI content struggles

### The adoption-to-results gap
- 64% of marketers use AI, only 18% report competitive advantage (HubSpot)
- "AI fails to deliver measurable value in 95% of cases." — Arthur Sadoun, CEO, Publicis
- Fewer than half of AI pilots are successfully scaled (IBM CMO study)

### Quality and brand voice
- 36% find maintaining brand voice challenging; 42% fear lack of originality (Brafton)
- 91% of marketers edit AI copy to make it sound human (Canva)
- AI-generated posts get 45% less engagement (Originality.AI)
- Consumer enthusiasm for AI content dropped from 60% to 26% between 2023-2025 (eMarketer)

### Overwhelm and pressure
- 70% of marketers feel overwhelmed by pace of AI developments (SOCi)
- 42% have received zero AI training (SOCi)
- 44% say new AI tools caused confusion, not efficiency (SOCi)
- 84.9% experience imposter syndrome, intensified for half (Marketing Week)
- "There's a gap where everyone feels the pressure to use AI, but there isn't always a structure around it."

### The "do more with less" mandate
- Marketing budgets flat at 7.7% of revenue; only 24% of CMOs have enough budget (CMSWire)
- 69% of CMOs say leadership demands measurable results for everything — up 59% from two years ago
- "CEOs and boards believe AI will drive growth quickly — within six to twelve months. Marketing teams are being asked to adopt new technology, create growth, and take on new responsibilities."

### AEO panic
- Zero-click searches: 56% (2024) → 69% (2025)
- AI Overviews reduce clicks by 58% as of early 2026
- "AEO is generally a mystery to businesses and marketers alike." (HubSpot)
- One marketer described "a professional identity crisis due to answer engine optimization"

## Key language patterns (for copy use)

**Words prospects actually use:**
- "slow" / "really slow"
- "overwhelmed"
- "steep learning curve"
- "not a slam dunk"
- "is that really going to happen?"
- "confused"
- "figuring it out"
- "debugging"
- "heavy editing"

**Emotional states:**
- Fear of wasting money on a tool that won't deliver
- Pressure from above to make AI work, no structure for how
- Overwhelmed by pace of change
- Skeptical that quality is achievable
- Relief when burden is lifted ("you can run through our environment")
