## Category clichés (banned words)

### Tier 1 — used by nearly everyone
- "scalable" / "at scale"
- "end-to-end"
- "revenue engine" / "growth engine"
- "ambitious brands"
- "drive/driven growth"
- "organic growth"
- "AI-powered"
- "elite" (strategists/team)
- "for the cost of one in-house hire"
- "content that ranks"
- "dominate"
- "trusted by"
- "measurable results"

### Tier 2 — used by 3-4 competitors
- "next generation" / "new breed"
- "move the needle"
- "extension of your team"
- "bottlenecks"
- "secret sauce"
- "winning content"
- "forward-deployed"
- "unmissable"
- "proprietary AI workflows"
- "compounding growth"
- "auto-publishing"

### Structural clichés
- "You get X for the cost of Y" (TACO, Depends, Apex)
- "We handle everything so you can focus on..." (Apex, Depends, GrowthX)
- "Most agencies do X (bad). We do Y (good)." (TACO, Depends)
- Four-bullet differentiator block (TACO, Depends, Apex — eerily similar wording)
- "[stat] + [stat] + [stat]" social proof lead (everyone)

## Key observations

1. **The partner ecosystem is a monoculture.** Everyone sounds the same — same words, same structures, same positioning.
2. **TACO and Depends are copy-paste twins.** Shared template or same parent org.
3. **Everyone is SEO/AEO-focused.** Nobody talks about content quality, thought leadership, voice calibration, or editorial standards.
4. **Nobody addresses what can go wrong.** Zero partners discuss risk, failure modes, or AI content quality challenges. Trust gap.
5. **GrowthX is the most differentiated** — visible pricing, "who we're NOT for" section, system metaphor.
6. **Nobody positions as content-first.** This is Animalz's open lane.
