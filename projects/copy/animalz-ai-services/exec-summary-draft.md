# AI Services Landing Page: Creative Direction

**Page:** animalz.co/ai-services (full landing page)
**Audience:** Content/marketing leaders (VP, Director, CMO) at B2B SaaS companies who are evaluating or stuck with AirOps. Under pressure from leadership and peers to make AI work for content — while also facing growing AEO demands. Worried about spending big on a tool that doesn't deliver and having their reputation damaged if the investment fails.

## Strategy

### The market situation

AirOps just raised at a billion-dollar valuation. Content and marketing leaders are hearing about it from their teams, seeing the billboards, and feeling pressure to act. But the experience after purchase is often disappointment: "It's really slow," "zero onboarding," "2-3 hours of editing per article." The current AirOps partner ecosystem (Team Empathy, Apex, TACO, Depends, Contact Studios, GrowthX) is entirely SEO/AEO-focused. None bring deep content operations expertise combined with technical building capability. They all use the same language — "scalable," "end-to-end," "revenue engine," "ambitious brands" — and none talk about content quality.

The content-first lane is wide open.

### What we're selling

Animalz builds AI content systems for B2B content teams — centered on AirOps workflow engineering and the surrounding infrastructure that makes it all work.

**This is an AirOps implementation play.** We position as the premier AirOps implementation partner for content teams, with SEO optimization targeting "AirOps implementation partner," "AirOps workflow services," and "AirOps consulting." Short-term: lean into the AirOps wave. Long-term: vendor-agnostic premium consultancy.

**The skills gap.** Most content teams can't build AI content systems because it requires a fundamentally different skill set: data pipelines, workflow architecture, API integrations, system design. Your content team doesn't have these skills and doesn't want to learn them. You either hire builders who also understand content (hard to find) or work with a partner who has both.

**Beyond the workflow.** Getting the core AirOps workflow right is step one. But AI content systems require much more:
- Data infrastructure (connected databases, API integrations, data pipelines)
- Experience layer (review interfaces, dashboards, intake forms, style calibrators)
- Integrations (engagement data feeding back in, trending topics triggering workflows)
- Organizational change (new roles, processes, quality definitions — where most AI projects die)

This is the AI Onion insight: a workflow touches everything around it. AirOps can help set up a basic workflow, but they don't build everything else.

**Cost advantage.** Teams can run through our AirOps environment — no separate subscription, no setup cost. This is a meaningful differentiator for buyers hesitant about committing to yet another platform.

## Three creative concepts

Each concept is presented as a standalone creative direction for the landing page. They are potentially compatible but should be evaluated independently.

### Concept A: The Secret

**Hero headline:**
Some of our clients won't even tell their board how their content gets made.

**The idea:** Everyone in AI content is hiding that AI wrote it because the output is mediocre. Our clients have the opposite problem — the content from our AirOps workflows is so good they're guarding the process like a trade secret. The workflows we build become competitive advantage, not just a tool. This flips the default AI content narrative on its head.

**Why it works:**
- Visualizable: picture the boardroom, the guarded conversation
- Falsifiable: either the content is that good or it isn't
- Nobody else: requires actual production-quality AirOps systems to back up — the SEO/AEO shops can't make this claim
- Surprise at end of sentence (Whipple): "won't tell their board" sets up a scandal, the reveal is that the content is too good to share

**How it connects to what we sell:**
The secrecy implies a system worth guarding. The page would reveal what that system is: AirOps workflows + data infrastructure + integrations + org change — the full AI Onion. The secrecy concept frames our technical implementation work as something that produces results so good they become proprietary advantage.

**Extends into:**
- "The first rule of your AI content system: don't talk about your AI content system."
- "Our workflows come with an NDA. (Not really. But you'll want one.)"
- "You'll have some explaining to do when your AI-generated content is this good."
- "Workflows are the new IP (if you build them right)."
- The broader idea that AI content workflows are a competitive moat — especially when built by people who understand content quality, not just automation.

**Supporting variant:**
"This is NOT AI generated." — most content marketers.
"This IS AI generated." — you, after working with Animalz.

### Concept B: The Publish Button

**Hero visual + copy:**
[Image: A single publish button, visibly worn out — cracked, faded, showing heavy use]
Your publish button after six weeks with Animalz.

**The idea:** Instead of promising abstract outcomes, show a physical object that tells the story. The worn-out publish button communicates volume, speed, and quality (you wouldn't publish if it were bad) in one image. It's a visual shorthand for: "Our AirOps workflows produce so much publishable content that keeping up is your new problem." This directly addresses the audience's biggest fear — that AI tools produce content that never makes it to publish because it's not good enough.

**Why it works:**
- Instantly visualizable: one image tells the whole story
- Falsifiable: either you're publishing that much or you're not
- Nobody else: no competitor in this space uses humor + visual storytelling — they all use generic B2B growth language
- Platform potential: can extend into a campaign series — "your editorial calendar after Animalz," "your style guide after Animalz," "your review queue after Animalz" — all showing objects worn out from overuse
- Relatable: every content team knows the pain of content that never makes it to publish, especially AI-generated content

**How it connects to what we sell:**
The worn button is the *result* of what we build. The page below the hero reveals how we get there: AirOps implementation, data infrastructure, integrations, fast feedback loops. The visual earns the right to make concrete claims — "same-day first drafts," "0-1 revision rounds," "185K+ tasks processed."

**Extends into:**
- Billboard / social ad / LinkedIn campaign
- Series of "after six weeks" visuals showing content-related objects pushed to their limits
- Conference/event materials
- A natural bridge to proof points and metrics

### Concept C: Knowing Which Half

**Hero headline:**
Half your content can be written by AI.
The trouble is knowing which half.

**The idea:** Riffs on the classic advertising quote ("Half my advertising spend is wasted; the trouble is I don't know which half"). Positions Animalz as the strategic expert who knows where AI works and where it doesn't. This speaks to a buyer who's further upstream — they haven't fully committed to AI content yet and need the strategic frame before they need the build.

**Why it works:**
- Visualizable: picture a split — half/half
- Falsifiable: it's a provably true claim about the current state of AI content
- Nobody else: requires deep knowledge of both content quality AND AI capability — the rare combination
- Familiar structure: echoes a famous marketing line, which makes it feel smart and credible to the target audience (marketing leaders will get the reference)

**How it connects to what we sell:**
This concept leads directly into the strategic layer of our offering — the "honest about human-AI roles" positioning pillar. It frames the Advise tier ($25-50K audit) naturally: "Let's figure out which half for you." It also bridges to AirOps implementation: "Once we know which half, we build the system that handles it."

**Extends into:**
- "We don't promise 100% AI production. We don't pretend humans need to do most of the work. The answer depends on the content type."
- Section about honest human-AI roles
- Natural entry point for the Advise tier
- Consultation CTA: "Let's figure out which half for you."

## Recommendation

These are three distinct creative directions. Each could anchor the full landing page independently.

**Concept B (Publish Button)** is the most visually distinctive and the most different from everything else in the market. No competitor in this space uses humor, visual storytelling, or physical metaphors. It immediately separates Animalz from the cold/techy B2B aesthetic. Strongest as a campaign platform.

**Concept A (The Secret)** is the strongest single headline. It does the most emotional work in the fewest words and creates immediate intrigue. Strongest as a brand-building direction.

**Concept C (Knowing Which Half)** is the most strategic and the most credible to a skeptical buyer. It positions expertise rather than just promising results. Strongest for building trust with senior decision-makers.
