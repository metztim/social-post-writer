# Visual Prompts for Nano Banana Pro

Use these in Gemini. Attach page 2 (Colors) of the Animalz brand guidelines as an image — this is the only page needed for color/mood reference. The PDF can't be attached directly, so screenshot or export that page.

**Round 2 approach:** Realistic photographs with no text on the image. Text is added as captions in Notion alongside the image. 16:9 landscape aspect ratio, start at 2K resolution.

**Important instruction to include in every prompt:** "This is a standalone photograph that will be paired with a separate text caption. Do not include any text, words, logos, labels, or typography anywhere in the image."

## Concept A: The Secret — Boardroom photograph

**Reference images to attach:**
1. Brand guidelines colors page (page 2) — for warm palette and purple accent reference

**Prompt:**

This image is for an ad campaign for a content marketing agency. The ad's message is: "Some of our clients won't even tell their board how their content gets made." The image needs to visually tell the story of someone guarding a secret in a boardroom. This is a standalone photograph that will be paired with a separate text caption. Do not include any text, words, logos, labels, or typography anywhere in the image.

Generate a realistic photograph in 16:9 landscape format of a corporate boardroom during a meeting. The composition is centered on one person who is the clear focal point of the image. This person is standing or half-standing at the table, holding a leather briefcase tightly behind their back with both hands, arms wrapped around it — an exaggerated, theatrical hiding gesture, like a kid caught with stolen cookies. Their expression is a forced casual smile, trying too hard to look innocent. The briefcase should be large and clearly visible behind them — not tucked away, but obviously being concealed.

The other four or five people around the conference table are completely absorbed in the meeting — looking at laptops, taking notes, mid-conversation — totally oblivious to the person's ridiculous hiding act. This contrast is the joke.

The person hiding the briefcase should be positioned at the center of the frame. Shoot it like a comedy scene — the audience is in on the secret. Warm, golden-hour lighting through large windows. Modern boardroom with subtle purple accents in the decor (chair cushions, a folder, wall art). The mood is conspiratorial and funny — not subtle, not stock photo.

**Notion caption:** "Some of our clients won't even tell their board how their content gets made."

## Concept B: The Publish Button — CMS button on screen

**Reference images to attach:**
1. Brand guidelines colors page (page 2) — for purple brand color (hex 9463EE)

**Prompt:**

This image is for an ad campaign for a content marketing agency. The ad's message is: "Your publish button after six weeks with Animalz." The joke is that the client is publishing so much content that the button itself is worn out. The image needs to make the "worn out from overuse" immediately obvious and funny. This is a standalone photograph that will be paired with a separate text caption. Do not include any text, words, logos, labels, or typography anywhere in the image.

Generate a realistic photograph in 16:9 landscape format showing a close-up of a laptop keyboard and screen. The focal point is the area of the screen where a large, prominent CMS-style publish button would be — but the screen is cracked and caved in right at that spot, as if someone has been hammering it with their finger thousands of times. The crack radiates outward from a single impact point. The rest of the screen is pristine.

Alternatively: show a laptop where a single key on the keyboard (where a "publish" shortcut would be) is completely worn down — the letter rubbed off, the key visibly lower than all the others, the plastic cracked or discolored from obsessive use. Every other key looks brand new.

The laptop sits on a clean, modern desk. Warm natural lighting. Shallow depth of field focused on the damage. The mood should be immediately funny — the exaggerated physical evidence of someone who publishes way too much content. Think comedy, not subtlety.

**Notion caption:** "Your publish button after six weeks with Animalz."

## Concept C: Knowing Which Half — Two screens, one person

**Reference images to attach:**
1. Brand guidelines colors page (page 2) — for warm palette and color reference

**Prompt:**

This image is for an ad campaign for a content marketing agency. The ad's message is: "Half your content can be written by AI. The trouble is knowing which half." The image needs to show someone genuinely stumped trying to tell two things apart. This is a standalone photograph that will be paired with a separate text caption. Do not include any text, words, logos, labels, or typography anywhere in the image.

Generate a realistic photograph in 16:9 landscape format of a person sitting at a desk in a modern office, facing two large monitors side by side. We see them from a three-quarter angle so both screens and the person's expression are visible.

Both monitors display what looks like content or documents — but here's the key: both screens look almost identical. Both show clean, professional-looking content. The visual joke is that you can't tell the difference either. The person is throwing their hands up in exasperation, or has their head in their hands, or is looking back and forth between the screens with an exaggerated "I give up" expression. The body language should be theatrical and immediately readable — not a subtle head tilt, but a clear comedic reaction.

The office has warm, natural lighting. The desk is clean and modern. The overall mood is comedic frustration — the person has been staring at these two screens for hours and still can't tell which is AI-generated. Think comedy, not corporate stock photo.

**Notion caption:** "Half your content can be written by AI. The trouble is knowing which half."

## Notes

- **No text on images.** All copy goes in Notion captions alongside the photograph.
- Each prompt includes the ad concept as context so the model understands the story, plus an explicit no-text guardrail.
- Push for theatrical, exaggerated compositions — these need to land as visual punchlines at a glance, not subtle details you have to search for.
- Start at 2K resolution, upscale to 4K after approving the base composition.
- Iterate: first pass for composition and mood, second pass for detail refinement.
- If the model adds text despite the instruction, follow up with: "Regenerate the same scene but remove all text, words, and labels from the image. The image should be purely photographic with no typography."
