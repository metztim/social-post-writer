# Remote-to-Real: Meeting a Virtual Coworker IRL

**Status:** Draft v2
**Date:** 2026-03-04

---

Five years at Animalz. Never met a single person I work with.

Not a coworker. Not my boss. Not a customer. Nobody.

Last Sunday I picked up Johnson—first person on my AI team—at the airport in Koh Samui. By coincidence, it was the exact day of my five-year anniversary. (Didn't even realize until the next morning.)

My mind was preparing for a stranger. A year of working closely together, but only through screens.

But we weren't strangers. Within a minute we were joking about something.

The humor, the shorthand, the way we talk—all of it fully formed from working together remotely. Nothing needed adjusting.

The only thing that took a moment was the physical presence. On Zoom you see someone from the shoulders up. In person they're suddenly standing there—and they're always slightly different than you imagined.

What surprised me was not that it went well. It was how little the physical dimension mattered.

I had the same experience years ago, meeting Josh Catone after building Saint together for about a year. Same pattern—within minutes, it felt like picking up from our last call, except in the same room.

If you've met a close remote coworker in person for the first time—what was it like for you?
