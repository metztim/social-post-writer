# LinkedIn post: ActiveCampaign article

[**Ana Cvetković**](https://www.linkedin.com/feed/#) interviewed me about avoiding AI slop at work and somehow extracted a coherent framework from my rambling. The highlights:

Build infrastructure first.  
Don't just plug ChatGPT into your workflow. Create databases that give AI context, build brand kits that define voice and strategy, and establish clear handoff points between AI and humans.

Use feedback loops.  
Store outputs, analyze what works, and feed that learning back into your prompts and brand kits.

Apply clear editing principles.  
Use frameworks like MECE and DRY to cut slop systematically.

Watch for slop everywhere.  
Internal docs, briefs, and memos are just as vulnerable to sloppification as external content. Most people aren't trying to cheat, they just want a shortcut. AI makes shortcuts dangerously easy.

Know where AI adds value and where it doesn't.  
At [**Animalz**](https://www.linkedin.com/feed/#), we use plenty of AI for LinkedIn and AEO audits, minimal AI for thought leadership.
